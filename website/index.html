Biography

Prof. Dr. Saba Irshad is a distinguished academic and researcher at the School of Biochemistry and Biotechnology, University of the Punjab. She holds a Ph.D. in Biochemistry/Molecular Biology from Quaid-e-Azam University (QAU), Islamabad, where she was also awarded a Gold Medal for her M.Phil research.

She further specialized in the molecular genetics of rare genetic disorders during her Post-Doctoral fellowship at Columbia University, New York, USA. As an HEC Approved Supervisor, she has mentored over 8 Ph.D. and 40 M.Phil scholars.

Research Focus

Rare Genetic Disorders

Investigating the molecular basis of rare hereditary conditions prevalent in the Pakistani population to aid in diagnosis and management.

Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Unraveling the genetic complexities behind neurodevelopmental challenges, focusing on identifying novel mutations.

Molecular Genetics of Deafness

Comprehensive study of families with hereditary hearing loss to map disease genes and understand pathophysiology.
