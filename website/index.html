About Me

I am a Professor at the School of Biochemistry and Biotechnology, University of the Punjab, Lahore. With a strong foundation in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, I have dedicated my career to teaching, research, and the mentorship of future scientists.

I completed my Ph.D. in Biochemistry/Molecular Biology from the Department of Biological Sciences, Quaid-e-Azam University (QAU), Islamabad, where I also earned my M.Phil (Gold Medalist) and M.Sc degrees. Further honing my expertise, I completed a one-year Post-doctorate at Columbia University, New York, USA, focusing on the molecular genetics of rare genetic disorders.

Research Interests

My research work primarily spans Genetics and Genomics. I am actively involved in collaborative research with international scientists, medical professionals, and affected families.

Rare Genetic Disorders

Investigation of molecular genetics underlying rare genetic conditions in the local population.

Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Research into the genetic basis of neurodevelopmental conditions.

Molecular Genetics of Deafness

Study of hereditary hearing loss families to identify genetic mutations.

Genetic Counseling

Working with affected families for genetic counseling and disease awareness.
